Blue-and-Yellow Macaw

Life Cycle:

Breeding: Blue-and-Yellow Macaws breed once a year, typically during the early part of the year. They usually nest in cavities of large trees.

Eggs: The female typically lays 2-3 eggs, which are incubated for about 28 days.

Chicks: After hatching, the chicks remain in the nest for about 3 months, during which both parents feed and care for them. The young macaws depend on their parents for food until they learn to fly.

Lifespan: In the wild, these macaws can live for 30-35 years. In captivity, with proper care, they can live up to 50-60 years.
